What the heck is a Bullet Journal?

The Bullet Journal is described as "The Analog System for the Digital Age." It is a self-designed agenda/journal/life-tracker, invented by Ryder Carroll, a digital product designer.

The traditional Bullet Journal is kept in a dot-grid notebook. Its most basic purpose is as a simple but effective planner, tracking events, tasks, and notes. You may also use it as a visual journal, habit and mindfulness tracker, scrapbook, or a combination of all of the above

I have been using a Bullet Journal since 2016. I combined the Bullet Journal's traditional habit and productivity tracking with the Pomodoro Technique featured in another analog system, the Productivity Planner. The two techniques combined are the makeup of the base version of Bull-It.

Isn't there already an app for that?

There is a Bullet Journal app, as well as countless pomodoro timer apps available. I have used them. But there currently are none that combine the two techniques as I need, and none as visually pleasing as Bull-It.
